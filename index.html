How to use ställer

There are numerous ways to use ställer, which can become confusing to new learners. Context is necessary to understand which to use. This is a guide to help those who are learning Swedish to better understand this frequently used verb.

Definition 1

to place or to put something in a particular location

till exempel: Jag ställer mina böcker på hyllan. (I put my books on the shelf.)

Definition 2

to arrange, organize, or prepare something

till exempel: Vi ställer i ordning på fest. (We are preparing the party.)

Definition 3

to ask a question or make a request of someone

till exempel: Kan jag ställa en fråga? (Can I ask a question?)

Definition 4

to pose, present, or take up a position on a particular issue or topic

till exempel: Han ställde sig positiv till förslaget. (He took a positive position on the proposal.)

Definition 5

to require or demand something of someone

till exempel: Jobbet ställer höga krav på oss. (The job requires/demands a lot from us)

Definition 6

to substitute or replace something for something else

till exempel: Jag ställde min cykel för bilen idag. (I substituted my bike for my car today.)

Definition 7

to cancel or postpone a meeting, event, or appointment

Vi tvingdades ställa in mötet på grund av sjukdom. (We had to cancel the meeting due to sickness.)

Definition 8

to go stand somewhere

Ställ dig där! (Go stand there!)

Han ställde sig längst fram på konserten. (He went up and stood at the very front at the concert.)

Definition 9

to get on one's feet, to get up

Han ställde sig upp, så nu står han. (He stood up, so now he is standing.)

Definition 10

to make someone speechless with confusion; to make someone lost for words

Hon blev helt ställd av hans uppträdande. (She was completely dumbfounded by his behavior.)

Definition 11

to set or adjust (a clock, a setting)

Han ställde fläkten på max. (He put the fan on the max setting.)

TLDR

Ställer is used in quite a few different ways and it can become confusing. One needs context to understand the meaning. Practice makes perfect!

  2. to arrange, organize, or prepare something
  3. to ask a question or make a request of someone
  4. to pose, present, or take up a position on a particular issue or topic
  5. to require or demand something of someone
  6. to substitute or replace something for something else
  7. to cancel or postpone a meeting, event, or appointment
  8. to go stand somewhere
